DST ABOLISHMENT

about a century ago in 1916 the practice of daylight savings time was put into effect, and it still stands today although not for good reason.



why?
  • Obsolescence. DST was made years ago and the energy standards and wartime conditions of the time don't hold up today.
  • Disruption. DST has been shown to have many negative impacts on human behaviour, like triggering seasonal depression.
  • Sleep loss. DST can throw off some people's sleep schedules, which may lead to a decrease in productivity.
  • Complication. Our modern society is a complex machine and suddenly changing the clock can make it so that not everyone's on the same page.
