Some other interesting statistics
  • Car crashes. Multiple studies have shown that when daylight savings time causes a transition sleep schedules can be altered, sleep can be lost, and thus car crashes increase
  • Crime. The falling back of the clock has been linked to an increase in crime, which has been linked to the change in sleep patterns. evidence of this is supplied in this study.
  • Energy savings. One of the original reasons for enacting DST during World War I was to conserve electricity, however modern society operates under a plethora of different circumstances like greater power efficiency now. One study shows that the power savings from DST may be as small as 0.34%, which doesn't even come close to balancing the cons which have been presented on our site.
  • Popular opinion. An EU poll determined that a staggering 84% of the EU population (4.6 million responses) wishes for the abolishment of DST. That is an enermous majority, and should not be ignored.
